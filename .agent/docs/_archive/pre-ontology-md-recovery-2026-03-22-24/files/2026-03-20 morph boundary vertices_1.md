Geometry should output a canonical boundary representation, not just polygons. Each territory frontier is a loop of identity-bearing boundary vertices and directed boundary edges. Each edge knows:  its unique ID,  previous/next edge IDs,  start/end vertex IDs,  left/right owner IDs,  whether it is owner-owner or owner-world,  its geometric definition to the next vertex, such as line, arc, or smoothed curve parameters.  Transition should diff canonical boundary entities, not raw sampled polygons. Given TMAP-A and TMAP-B, determine which vertices/edges are unchanged, which are deleted, which are inserted, and where the frontier diverges and rejoins. Those divergence/rejoin points are the transition anchors.  Animation should operate only on the changed boundary patch. For the changed arc in A and the changed arc in B, sample both arcs to equal counts along arc length, preserving order between anchors, then interpolate sample  i i in A to sample  i i in B. Unchanged edges are copied exactly on every frame.
